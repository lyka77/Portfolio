CS 220 Gratitude Project: Mapshots App


Team Members: Euphy Liu, Diana Padron, Sophie Sebastiani, Ariel Traver

The idea behind this app was to create an app that would cultivate gratitude within the Wellesley community. We chose to do this by creating a platform where people could save and share photographs from around campus, and connect to others in the community.


Initial Wireframe:


To begin, we sketched out a wireframe to display the overall function of our app and communicate our ideas. The wireframes were then presented to our classmates and they had the opportunity to give us feedback on them.


Low-fidelity Prototype:


Using the feedback we recieved from our wireframe, we used figma to create a low-fidelity prototype of all the different screens and interactions between them. The above pictures show some of these screens, displaying the home screen, the account profile, and the messaging and posting functions.

High-fidelity Prototype:


Then, using the feedback we recieved from the low-fidelity prototype, we used figma to create a high-fidelity prototype, which would be our final version of this project.
