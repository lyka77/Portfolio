CS 304 Final Website Project: Wellesley World


Team Members: Eleanor Braun, Chelsea Ji, Jada Onwuta
Class: CS304 Databases with Web Interfaces

This project was pretty open ended, we just had to come up with an idea for a website that we would then design, implement, and test. Our idea was to create a page where wellesley students and alumni could connect based on interest, location, and career aspirations in a casual and simple way. We used the tools we learned in this class such as html, css, javascript, EJS, and mongoDB to implement our site.

The stages to the development of our website were:

  • Proposal
  • Design & Implementation Plan
  • Draft Version
  • Alpha Version
  • Beta Version

After each stage, we got feedback from our professor and sometimes our peers that we applied in the next stage. The proposal and design parts of this project were tackled as a team, but once we moved on to implementation we divided the work of development up between the four of us, meeting occasionally and communicating to make sure it was cohesive. The parts I tackled were mainly the chat function/page and several design aspects like the look of the login and launchpage. I also tackled friending/unfriending, designed the logo, and worked alongside my teammates on some other aspects.
